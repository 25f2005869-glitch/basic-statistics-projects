
# STATISTICS 2 — Extra Activity 1

## Project: Netflix Movies & TV Shows Dataset (Basic Statistics)

### Objective
Use the Netflix Movies and TV Shows dataset to practice **basic statistical analysis** and extract meaningful insights about Netflix’s content library.

---

## Dataset Description

### Rows
Each row represents **one title** available on Netflix (either a **Movie** or a **TV Show**).  
Example: a row may represent the movie *Inception* or the TV series *Stranger Things*.

### Columns (Fields)
> Note: Column names may differ slightly depending on the dataset version.

- **Title**: Name of the movie/TV show.
- **Director**: Director of the title (can be blank if not available).
- **Country**: Country of production/origin.
- **Release Year**: Year the title was released.
- **Genre**: Category/type of the title (Drama, Comedy, Action, Documentary, etc.).

---

## Analysis & Key Points (What you can study)

### 1) Genre Distribution
- Count titles by **Genre** to see which categories appear the most.
- In many Netflix datasets, **Drama** is among the most common genres, reflecting broad audience demand.

### 2) Country Analysis
- Count titles by **Country** to identify the top content-producing countries.
- The **USA** and **India** often appear near the top, showing the influence of Hollywood and Bollywood.

### 3) Director Analysis
- Identify directors who appear multiple times.
- Repeated entries may indicate recurring collaborations or that Netflix hosts multiple works from the same director.

### 4) Trends Over Time (Release Year)
- Plot the number of titles by **Release Year** to observe production and acquisition trends.
- You may notice increases in content around **2013** and later, as Netflix expanded its original programming.
- Fluctuations can also reflect competition, changing viewer habits, or global events such as the **COVID-19** pandemic.

### 5) Genre Popularity Over Time / By Region
- Analyze how genres change across years.
- Compare genre distributions between countries/regions to understand global preferences.

### 6) Diversity in Production Countries
- Measure how many different countries are represented.
- Look for regional content trends (for example, growth of South Korean titles following global hits like *Squid Game*).

### 7) Ratings / Viewership (If Available)
If your dataset includes user ratings, review scores, or view counts:
- Compare performance across genres and countries.
- Identify which types of content correlate with higher engagement.

### 8) Netflix Originals vs Licensed Content (If Identifiable)
If Netflix Originals can be identified:
- Compare originals vs licensed content for genre diversity, release-year trends, and country distribution.

### 9) Language & Accessibility (If Included)
If language/subtitle fields exist:
- Study linguistic diversity and how Netflix serves non‑English-speaking audiences.

### 10) Long-Running Series vs Limited Series (If Seasons/Episodes Exist)
If the dataset includes seasons/episodes:
- Compare long-running shows with limited series/miniseries.
- Explore what format appears more frequently.

---

## Conclusion
This activity practices descriptive statistics (counts, frequencies, trends) using a real-world dataset. The analysis can reveal Netflix’s content strategy and global reach through patterns in genre, country, director repetition, and release-year trends.
